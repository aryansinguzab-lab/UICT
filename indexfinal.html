🧭 Navigation Controls
1
Open / Close the Sidebar

Tap the ☰ hamburger button (top-left corner of the map). It becomes ✕ when open. The map fills the full screen when the sidebar is hidden.

2
Detect Your Location

In the 📡 My Location panel, tap "Detect My Location on Campus". A green pulsing dot means GPS is active. A red message means location was denied or unavailable.

3
Enter Starting Point & Destination

In the 🧭 Get Directions panel, type in the Starting Point field. A dropdown of matching buildings and offices appears — select one. Then fill in the Destination field the same way. Tap 📍 Use my detected location to auto-fill your GPS position.

4
Swap Origin & Destination

Tap the ⇅ Swap button between the two fields to reverse your route instantly.

5
Choose Travel Mode

Select 🚶 On Foot for pedestrian walking speed, or 🚗 Official Route for vehicle speed. Time estimates adjust automatically.

6
Get Directions

Press the green Get Directions button or hit Enter. The 📋 Route Result panel shows total distance, estimated time, number of stops, and step-by-step directions.

🏛 Exploring the Campus
7
Tap Any Building on the Map

Click a building polygon or marker to open the 🏛 Building Info panel showing its name, type, description, and offices. Multi-floor buildings (e.g. Main Block) show Floor 1 / 2 / 3 tabs — tap a tab to see that floor's rooms.

8
Recently Viewed Places

The 🕐 Recently Viewed section tracks the last 6 buildings you tapped. Tap any entry to jump straight back to it. Use "Clear history" to reset.

9
Reset Map View

Tap the ⊙ button (top-right of the map) to snap back to the default campus overview at any time.

10
Switch Map Layers

Use the layer control (top-right, below ⊙) to toggle between satellite and street map tile styles.

🗺 Bottom Key Bar
11
Scrolling Colour Key

The animated bar at the bottom lists every building type with its colour dot. Hover over it to pause the scroll and read all categories.

🔴 Live Building Activities
12
Viewing Live Activities (Everyone)

When an activity is posted for a building, a red pulsing dot appears over that building on the map. Tap the building to see a 🔴 LIVE NOW banner inside the Building Info panel showing what is happening and the time window. Active events also scroll in the bottom key bar in pink.

13
Posting an Activity (Admins Only)

Tap the 📋 red button (floating, right side of map). The panel first shows any current live activities. To post or clear, enter the admin password and tap Unlock Admin Panel. Then select a building, type the activity description, set the Start Time and End Time, and tap Post Live Activity. The activity automatically disappears when the end time is reached — no manual removal needed.

✅ What This System Does Well
🔍
Fast Autocomplete SearchMatches building names, short names, offices, and room names as you type — fuzzy and instant.
🧮
Dijkstra PathfindingComputes the true shortest path through the campus road/walkway graph — not just a straight line between two points.
🚶🚗
Dual Travel ModesWalking vs. vehicle speed assumptions give realistic time estimates for both pedestrians and drivers.
🏢
Floor-Level Room DetailMulti-storey buildings expose individual floors and specific rooms — not just "get to the building."
📡
GPS Integration"Use my detected location" as a start point makes the tool practical for first-time visitors who don't know the campus layout.
🕐
Persistent Recent PlacesFrequently visited buildings are remembered across sessions via localStorage — one tap to revisit.
📱
Responsive Mobile LayoutAdapts to phone screens (≤680 px) by stacking the map below the sidebar panel.
👀
Built-in Calibration ModeThe 👀 button lets admins click any point on the map to read and copy its exact lat/lng — no GIS tools needed to update building positions.
🔴
Live Building Activity BoardAdmins can post real-time activities (lectures, exams, events) to any building with a start and end time. Activities auto-expire and are visible to all users via map markers, the info panel, and the scrolling key bar.
⚠️ Current Limitations to Be Aware Of
📍
Approximate Building CoordinatesPositions are manually estimated. Routes may not match real paths precisely until each building is physically verified with GPS on campus.
🔒
Static Path NetworkCampus paths are hardcoded. Temporary closures, construction detours, or new shortcuts require a manual code edit to reflect.
🚪
No Room-Level RoutingYou can route between buildings, not to a specific room number. Floor/room detail is informational only — the route ends at the building entrance.
🚗
Vehicle route limitationsSome points are un-reachable by vehicles hence requires parking the vehicles on designated parking yards and continue to the destination on foot
🌐
Map Tiles Require InternetSatellite and street map backgrounds need an internet connection. Without it, the map backdrop goes blank (though campus data still loads).
🚗
Vehicle Routing Uses Same Graph as WalkingThe "Official Route" mode changes speed only — it does not enforce one-way roads or vehicle-only lanes if those aren't mapped in the data.

💡 Tips for Best Results
💡
Type partial namesTyping "lab" or "admin" is enough — the autocomplete will suggest all matching buildings and rooms.
🗺️
Tap the map firstClicking a building on the map directly is often faster than typing — then use "Navigate here" from the info panel.
🔄
Collapse sections you don't needEach sidebar section has a ▾ toggle. Collapse sections like Recent Places to keep the Directions panel in view.
